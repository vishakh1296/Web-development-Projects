Housing

Seeking housing in Canada is one of the first things every immigrant needs to do on their to-do list. Depending on the situation, you may want to first consider temporary housing in Canada before looking for rental apartments or houses in Canada.

You can find houses and apartments for rent in Canada in the classifieds section of local newspapers, libraries, and on websites such as Rent Panda, Kijiji and Facebook groups for newcomers to Canada are also useful. In many cities, you can simply walk around your preferred neighborhood, as landlords will post signs outside their property advertising available vacancies.

Landlords would typically ask for clarification on your capacity to pay rent before extending a contract to you. Documents that could be submitted include a report from your new or more recent boss that shows your total income. Bank records that prove you have enough funds to pay the rent for a few months. References from former tenants.

Rent Panda

You can find the place to rent the ideal home where tenants and landlords can get all the reliable resources and information they need.

Kijiji

Kijiji is Canada's biggest classified platform with millions of live ads in a broad variety of categories-cars, homes, work and all in between. Two new advertisements are coming out every second! They are proud to be providing a forum that connects Canadians, helping them buy great things in their neighbourhood, make money out of discarded belongings that clutter homes, and help less waste the country.

Facebook

List all leased housing on the Marketplace of the Facebook group. All the sellers are genuine. It includes specifics such as the form of rental, price per month, number of bedrooms and bathrooms, square footage, friendliness of pets and more.
